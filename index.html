1.Bugatti Chiron: A successor to the Veyron, the Chiron is powered by an 8.0-liter quad-turbocharged W16 engine producing over 1,500 horsepower. It's known for its extreme speed and luxurious features.

2.Koenigsegg Jesko: With a 5.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine generating up to 1,600 horsepower, the Jesko aims to set new standards in the hypercar world with cutting-edge technology and aerodynamics.

3.Rimac C_Two: This all-electric hypercar boasts a massive 1,888 horsepower from its four electric motors and offers impressive acceleration and performance. It also features advanced autonomous driving capabilities.

4.McLaren Senna: Named after the legendary Formula 1 driver Ayrton Senna, this track-focused hypercar is powered by a 4.0-liter twin-turbo V8 engine producing 800+ horsepower and offers exceptional handling and aerodynamics.

5.Pagani Huayra: The Huayra combines art and engineering with a 6.0-liter twin-turbo V12 engine that produces around 750 horsepower. It's known for its stunning design, attention to detail, and exclusivity.

6.Aston Martin Valkyrie: A collaboration between Aston Martin and Red Bull Racing, the Valkyrie is designed for extreme track performance.

7.Lamborghini Sian: Lamborghini's first hybrid hypercar, the Sian combines a V12 engine with supercapacitor technology for extra power.

8.Bugatti La Voiture Noire: A one-of-a-kind hypercar with a stunning design and immense power, featuring a quad-turbocharged W16 engine.

9.Ferrari LaFerrari: Although no longer in production, the LaFerrari remains an icon with its hybrid powertrain and impressive performance.

10.Hennessey Venom F5: Promising to reach speeds over 300 mph, the Venom F5 features a V8 engine with over 1,800 horsepower.
